# Latest Updates - What's New

## Major Changes (Most Recent Update)

### 1. Philippine Peso Support
Your invoicing system now uses **Philippine Pesos (₱)** by default!
- All currency amounts display with ₱ symbol
- Default currency set to PHP
- Can be changed in Business Settings if needed

### 2. Tagalog Language Support
Use the system in **Tagalog** or English!

**How to change language:**
1. Go to Business Settings
2. Scroll to "Preferences" section
3. Select "Tagalog" from Language dropdown
4. Click "Save Changes"
5. All menu items and labels will switch to Tagalog

**Supported translations:**
- Menu navigation
- Form labels and buttons
- Invoice text
- Dashboard elements
- All system messages

### 3. Smarter Due Dates
Due date now defaults to **today** instead of 30 days out.
- Perfect for "due upon receipt" invoices
- You can still change it to a future date if needed
- Just click the Due Date field and pick a different date

### 4. Cleaner Unit Price Field
Unit price field is now cleaner:
- Doesn't show "0" when empty
- Shows "0.00" as a helpful placeholder
- Just start typing when you're ready to enter a price
- Works exactly the same, just looks better

### 5. Optional Customer Contact Info
Customer phone and email are now truly optional:
- **Phone field:** Won't print on invoice if left empty
- **Email field:** Won't print on invoice if left empty
- Only information you fill in appears on the invoice
- Makes invoices cleaner when you don't have contact details

### 6. Professional Invoice Look
Invoices now look more polished and professional:
- **Your business logo** is displayed at the top
- **Business details** (name, address, phone, etc.) appear on every invoice
- **Better spacing** and layout
- **Clear typography** and professional formatting
- **Preview shows exactly what prints** - no surprises

### 7. Business Logo on Invoices
Your business logo now displays on all invoices:
- Set it up in Business Settings
- Upload a square image (200x200px recommended)
- PNG or JPG format works best
- Logo appears on printed invoices too

## Step-by-Step: First Time Setup

### Step 1: Configure Your Business
1. Open the app
2. Click **Business Settings** (or use Tagalog equivalent)
3. Fill in your business information:
   - Business Name
   - Address
   - City & Postal Code
   - Phone & Email
   - Tax ID (optional)
4. **Upload Your Logo** - click the upload area
5. Set Tax/VAT Rate if applicable
6. Choose your Language (English or Tagalog)
7. Currency is already set to PHP ✓
8. Click **Save Changes**

### Step 2: Add Your Products
1. Go to **Inventory**
2. Click **Add Product**
3. Fill in:
   - Product Name
   - Cost Price (what you pay)
   - Selling Price (what you charge)
   - Quantity in Stock
   - Low Stock Alert (optional)
4. Click **Save**

### Step 3: Create Your First Invoice
1. Click **Create Invoice** in the menu
2. Enter customer name (required)
3. Add phone/email if you have them (optional - won't show if empty)
4. Dates are set to today (change if needed)
5. Click **Add Product** or **Add Labor**
6. Fill in items
7. Click **Preview** to see how it looks
8. Click **Create Invoice** to save

### Step 4: View & Print
1. Go to **Invoice History**
2. Find your invoice
3. Click **Eye icon** to view details
4. Click **Download** to save PDF
5. Click **Print icon** to print directly

## Common Tasks

### Task: Create Invoice Without Customer Phone
1. Enter customer name
2. **Skip phone field** (leave empty)
3. Add phone to email if you want (optional)
4. Phone won't appear on the printed invoice

### Task: Clear Unit Price and Re-enter
1. Click in Unit Price field
2. Select all (Ctrl+A or Cmd+A)
3. Delete
4. Field shows empty (not "0")
5. Type your price

### Task: Change Due Date
1. Create invoice form
2. Click Due Date field
3. Pick a different date from calendar
4. Or leave as today for "due upon receipt"

### Task: Add Your Logo
1. Business Settings
2. Logo section
3. Click "Upload Logo"
4. Select image file (PNG/JPG, 200x200px recommended)
5. Save Settings
6. Logo appears on all invoices

### Task: Switch to Tagalog
1. Business Settings
2. Scroll to Preferences
3. Language dropdown → Tagalog
4. Save Changes
5. Entire interface switches to Tagalog

## Tips & Tricks

💡 **Pro Tips:**
- Use today's date as due date for cash customers
- Upload a professional logo for better invoices
- Enter phone/email only if you plan to contact customer
- Check preview before creating invoice
- Unit price auto-fills when you select a product from inventory

⚠️ **Remember:**
- Once an invoice is created, it's saved forever
- You can mark invoices as "Paid" in History
- Use Low Stock Alert to keep track of inventory
- All data is saved locally on your computer
